Greg Zuerlein biography, height, wife, nicknames, salary, family

Posted by Kelle Repass on Wednesday, August 14, 2024

Greg Zuerlein is one of the best placekickers in the National Football League (NFL). He began his professional career in 2006 after being drafted by the St. Louis Rams that year. He started with the team at a high level, setting and beating existing records.

The 6-footer is so good in the game and has an exceptional ability to convert long field goals. In 2017, Greg’s talent and incredible performance earned him his first Pro Bowl and All-Pro honors.

Greg Zuerlein Biography (Nicknames)

A Nebraska native, Lincoln was born on December 27, 1987. He is known by the nicknames “Greg the Leg” or “Legatron” among both his fans and teammates. He earned these nicknames due to his ability to convert long field goals.

Greg Zuerlein was a student at Lincoln Pius X Catholic High School in Lincoln, Nebraska, and while there he played football and soccer and received letters in both. Additionally, he earned All-State and All-Class honors in his junior and senior years at Nebraska.

After graduating high school, Zürlein pursued his college football career at the University of Nebraska Omaha (UNO). He played three seasons but sat out the 2010 season due to a hip injury. While playing for the Omaha Mavericks football team, the talented player amassed a school record of 61 straight extra points and converted 19 of 26 field goal attempts.

In 2011, the UN scrapped its football program and as a result, Greg transferred to Missouri Western State University for his final college football season with the Missouri Western Griffons football team. There he compiled an NCAA Division II record of 21 consecutive field goals (seven of which were 50-yard and two of which were 58-yard field goals). He also had a .952 field goal percentage after completing 23 of 24 attempts. This was the highest by any NCAA player in a division with 12 attempts or more in 2011.

The kicker entered the 2012 NFL Draft and was selected by the St. Louis Rams in the 6th round with a 171st overall pick. This made him the first non-bowl kicker in the Championship Series since 2006 and the first kicker below the Division I Football Bowl subdivision since 2005.

He started his professional career as setting a very high standard for himself, having broken and set multiple league and franchise records in his rookie season. In Week 3 of the 2015 season, he hit a 56-yard field goal, which is Soldier Field’s longest.

In the game against the Seattle Seahawks the following week, he beat his own record of 58 yards, which he previously set in the same game with a 60-yard field goal. In doing so, he broke Jeff Wilkins’ 14-year record and became the first player in NFL history to hit a 60-yard streak. yard field goal and an over 50 yarder in the same game.

Also that same season, Greg hit a 54-yard field goal in week 13 during the game against the San Francisco 49ers and it was the second-longest field goal scored in overtime since the NFL began.

He joined the Los Angeles Rams in 2016 on a one-year contract, and in 2017 the contract was extended to the next three years. He has maintained his strong performances with the Rams and was named NFC Special Teams Player of the Month on November 2, 2017. In December 2017, he was named to his first Pro Bowl. The player finished the 2017 season as the league’s top scorer with 158 points and 11.3 points per game.

The 2019 season was again spectacular for Greg Zuerlein. He was instrumental in the Rams’ multi-game NFC Championship Game win over the New Orleans Saints. During the game, he hit all four of his field goal attempts and paved the way for his team to participate in Super Bowl LIII.

salary

In 2017, the placekicker signed a 3-year contract extension with the Los Angles Rams. The deal includes a signing bonus of $750,000, a guaranteed bonus of $2,375,000 and an average annual salary of $2,250,000. Zürlein will receive a base salary of $2,325,000 and a signing bonus of $250,000 in 2019. Consequently, the NFL star is expected to bring home a cap hit of $2,575,000 and a dead cap of $250,000.

family, woman

Greg Zuerlein is married to Megan Lynn Zuerlein(nee Krefels). Megan was born in Lincoln, Nebraska, USA in 1989 and is the last of three children born to Terry and Barbara Krefels. Her older siblings are named Amanda and Justin.

She attended Lincoln Pius X Catholic High School in Lincoln, Nebraska, and later transferred to Hastings College, where she worked as a goaltender for the girls’ soccer team. She was so good at sports that she won the 2011 Lincoln Journal Star Athlete of the Year. Megan later attended Creighton University, where she studied nursing. After graduating, she worked at St. Anthony’s Medical Center before going to Gateway Endoscopy Center.

Greg and his wife were high school sweethearts. They married on April 27, 2013 and are now blessed with four beautiful children. The first named Emma was born in 2014 while the second Morgan arrived on June 14, 2015. The couple welcomed their first son, named Greg Jr., in August 2016 and their second son, Samuel Gene, was born on November 12, 2018.

Height

Greg Zuerlein will surely rank among the tallest players in the NFL as his height is quite impressive. The Nebraska-born athlete is measured to be 6 feet (183 cm) tall. The NFL star also maintains a body weight of 87 kg (191 lb), which is consistent with his height.
