Early Life

Josh Peck was born on November 10, 1986, at New York City. His mother, Barbara, was a career coach. After his schooling, he studied in The Professional Performing Art School, New York City, where he got his initial training as an actor. He had a knack for comedy and accordingly his first appearance was as a comedian.

In June 2017 he married his long-time friend Paige O’Brien.

Age, Height, and Weight

Career

Josh Peck started his formal career as a stand-up comedy at Carolines Comedy Club for Audrey Hepburn Foundation. When he was thirteen, he got a role in Nickelodeon’s ‘The Amanda Show’ which was a success, and he became quite popular among young audiences.

He moved to Los Angeles where his first film in which he got an acting assignment was ‘Snow Day’ (2000). He starred in film ‘Max Keeble’s Big Move’ (2001) which was a theatrical film. His co-stars were Alex D. Linz and Zena Gray.

He appeared in the film ‘Spun’ and ‘Mean Creek’ and was widely appreciated. He starred in Nickelodeon sitcom ‘Drake and Josh’ (2004) which earned him a respected place among young audiences, He played as a smart young guy who was tormented by Drake’s younger sister.

In 2006 Josh Peck and Drake again starred in their own movie ‘Drake and Josh Go Hollywood’, and after its great success, the duo starred again in ‘Drake and Josh: Really Big Shrimp. In 2008 he was nominated as a ‘Favourite television actor’ by Nickelodeon Kid’s Choice Awards for his work ‘Drake and Josh’. His first directorial work was in a ‘Drake and Josh’ episode ‘Battle of Panthatar’.

In 2008 he again returned to the character of Josh in a TV film ’Merry Christmas, Drake and Josh’. In 2006 he voiced Eddie in the film ‘Special’. He played a school bully in the film ‘Drill Bit Taylor’ in 2008 and appeared in two more films ‘The Wackness’ and ‘American Primitive Safety Glass’ in the same year.

In 2011 he had a cameo role in ‘Victorious’, and in 2013 he voiced Casey Jones in ‘Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles’.

In 2015 Josh Peck was cast in ‘Grandfathered’ a comedy series along with John Stamos for which he got a nomination for 2016 People’s Choice Awards.
